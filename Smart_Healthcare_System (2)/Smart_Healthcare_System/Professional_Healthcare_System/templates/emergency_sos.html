Quick First Aid Guide
1. Check if the person is responsive – tap shoulder, shout.
2. Call 112 immediately or ask someone else to call.
3. Begin chest compressions – push hard at center of chest, 30 times.
4. Give 2 rescue breaths (if trained to do so).
5. Continue until help arrives or person recovers.
1. Ask "Are you choking?" – If they can't speak or breathe, act immediately.
2. Bend them forward and give 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades.
3. If ineffective, give 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
4. Alternate back blows and abdominal thrusts until object is expelled.
1. Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
2. Do not remove the cloth if it gets soaked – add more on top.
3. Elevate the injured area above heart level if possible.
4. Seek medical help if bleeding doesn't stop within 10 minutes.
1. Cool the burn under cool running water for at least 10 minutes.
2. Do NOT apply ice, butter, toothpaste, or any cream.
3. Cover loosely with a clean, non-fluffy material.
4. Seek medical help for burns larger than a palm size.
